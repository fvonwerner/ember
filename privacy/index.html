Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 14, 2026

The short version: your screen time is measured by iOS and stays on your phone. Spare never uploads your hours, your app list, or anything you look at. Friends see only what the app shows them: your name and tag, your daily limit, your streak, your day outcomes, and your lockdown time.

Who we are

Spare is made by Friedrich von Werner. For privacy questions, use the contact published on Spare's App Store page.

Screen time data stays on your device

Spare uses Apple's Screen Time frameworks (Family Controls, Device Activity, Managed Settings) to measure your usage of the apps you choose and to shield them when your daily limit is reached. This data is processed entirely on your device. Spare has no server that receives it, and we never use it for advertising or share it with anyone. Apple's frameworks are designed so that even Spare itself sees only coarse threshold events, not your browsing.

What syncs when you use the circle

If you add friends, Spare stores a small profile in Apple's iCloud (CloudKit), so your circle can see it:

  • your display name and user tag (chosen by you — no email, no phone number),
  • your daily limit in minutes,
  • your current streak and how each recent day ended (kept, kept with help, over, unverified),
  • your voluntary lockdown minutes per week (time you chose to put the phone down),
  • friendships, nudges, and partner requests/answers you send.

Like other apps built on CloudKit's shared public database, these profile basics are technically readable by other users of the app, not only your circle. They never include your usage hours or your app list.

Never synced: your hours of usage, which apps you track, or anything about what you do on your phone. Lockdown time is a timer you started on purpose — not a measurement of your usage.

Where it lives

Circle data is stored in Apple CloudKit, tied to your iCloud account, under Apple's security and data-processing terms. Spare has no separate backend and no analytics or tracking SDKs. There are no ads in the current version; if a future version shows optional rewarded ads, they will never receive your screen-time data.

Deleting your data

"Start over" in Spare's settings deletes your profile and your friendship records from CloudKit and wipes the app's local data. Deleting the app removes all on-device data. A small recovery backup (your name, tag, limit, streak and recent day outcomes) lives in your PRIVATE iCloud database, so reinstalling can restore your account; "Start over" deletes that backup too.

Your rights

Depending on where you live (for example under the GDPR), you may have rights to access, correct, or erase personal data. The circle data described above is the complete inventory; you can see it in the app and erase it with "Start over" at any time, or contact us via the App Store page.
