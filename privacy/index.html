Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

The short version: your screen time is measured by iOS and stays on your phone. Circle is optional. If you use it, each friendship gets a separate private Apple CloudKit share. Friends never see your hours, tracked apps, or other friends.

Who we are

Spare is made by Friedrich von Werner. For privacy questions, use the contact published on Spare's App Store page.

Screen time data stays on your device

Spare uses Apple's Screen Time frameworks (Family Controls, Device Activity, Managed Settings) to measure your usage of the apps you choose and to shield them when your daily limit is reached. This data is processed entirely on your device. Spare has no server that receives it, and we never use it for advertising or share it with anyone. Apple's frameworks are designed so that even Spare itself sees only coarse threshold events, not your browsing.

What syncs when you use the circle

If you choose to use Circle, Spare creates a separate private/shared Apple CloudKit zone for each friend you invite. Apple asks that person to accept before either of you appears in the other's Circle. A pair share contains:

  • your display name and an app-generated account identifier (no email or phone number),
  • your daily limit in minutes,
  • your current streak and how each recent day ended (kept, kept with help, over, unverified),
  • your voluntary lockdown minutes per week (time you chose to put the phone down),
  • nudges, session invitations and responses, and lifeline requests/answers you deliberately send.

Circle data is not searchable and is not stored in CloudKit's public database. Each accepted friend can access only the share created for that friendship.

Never synced: your hours of usage, which apps you track, or anything about what you do on your phone. Lockdown time is a timer you started on purpose — not a measurement of your usage.

Where it lives

Circle data is stored in Apple's private/shared CloudKit databases, tied to your iCloud account, under Apple's security and data-processing terms. Spare has no separate backend and no analytics or tracking SDKs. There are no ads in the current version; if a future version shows optional rewarded ads, they will never receive your screen-time data.

Deleting your data

"Delete account and data" in Spare's settings deletes every friend share you own, leaves shares owned by friends, deletes the private recovery record, and then wipes local data. If CloudKit deletion fails, Spare keeps the local identity and shows an error so you can retry. Deleting the app removes local data but a small private recovery backup may restore your display name, identifier, limit, streak, and recent day outcomes after reinstalling.

Safety, reports, and blocking

Spare filters the short user-authored strings it transports. You can report a safety concern, block an account, remove a friend, or revoke sharing from a friend's profile. Reports are prepared on your device for you to send through the system share sheet. A dedicated support contact will be published on this site and the App Store page before release.

Your rights

Depending on where you live (for example under the GDPR), you may have rights to access, correct, or erase personal data. The circle data described above is the complete inventory; you can see it in the app and erase it with "Start over" at any time, or contact us via the App Store page.
